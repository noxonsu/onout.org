Cloud GPU Providers in 2023: A Comparison of Features and Prices

Introduction

Cloud GPU services have become increasingly popular in recent years, offering users the ability to harness the power of high-performance graphical processing units (GPUs) without the need for expensive on-premises hardware. These services provide a range of features and pricing options to cater to various use cases, from AI and deep learning to graphic-intensive applications.

In this article, we will compare the features and prices of cloud GPU offerings from various providers. We have compiled a list of industry features, including hardware specifications, performance and scalability, connectivity and networking, security and compliance, support and service, software and applications, service management, pricing and availability, data center and infrastructure, and additional services. We will examine how these features vary across providers and their corresponding price points.

Cloud GPU Providers Comparison

Now, let's compare the features and prices of cloud GPU offerings from various providers:

Cherry Servers

Cherry Servers offers a range of Nvidia GPU accelerators, including the Nvidia A2 Tensor Core, Nvidia A10 Tensor Core, Nvidia A16 Tensor Core, Nvidia Tesla P4, Nvidia Quadro K2200, and Nvidia Quadro K4200. They also provide custom GPU requests and single-tenant bare metal servers. Their pricing starts at $0.13 per hour or $68 per month for the Nvidia Quadro K2200 and goes up to $0.53 per hour or $252 per month for the Nvidia A10 Tensor Core. Additionally, they offer up to 100TB of free monthly traffic.

Server Room

Server Room offers a variety of server configurations with different GPU models and memory options. Their pricing starts at €62.18 per month for the Intel Xeon E3-1284L v4 Quad Core with Intel Iris Pro P6300 GPU and 8GB RAM. They also offer instant deployment, fast network, and 24/7 support.

GPU Mart

GPU Mart offers a variety of GPU models for different use cases, including GeForce GT730, Quadro K620, and Quadro P600. Their pricing starts at $14.00 per month for the Express GPU VPS-GT730 with 8GB RAM and goes up to $399.00 per month for the Enterprise GPU-RTX A6000 with 256GB RAM, Dual 18-Core E5-2697v4, and Nvidia Quadro RTX A6000. They also provide savings on select plans.

GCore

GCore offers powerful graphics cards for processing and training neural networks, such as the Nvidia A100 and Nvidia V100. They offer a range of server configurations with different GPU and memory options. Their pricing starts at €15.52 per hour or €11,174.4 per month for the bm3-ai-large-a100-40-4 configuration with 4xA100 GPUs, 40GB GPU memory, and 2TB RAM. They also provide high-speed Infiniband interconnect for fast data transfer.

VelociHost

VelociHost offers GPU servers with NVIDIA GTX 1060. Their pricing starts at $189 per month for the GPU server with 6 Cores / 12 Threads, 32 GB RAM, 250 GB SSD, and 20 TB Transfer. They also offer instant deployment and 1 Gbps port speed. Additionally, they provide a range of server types including VPS and dedicated servers.

Binary Racks

Binary Racks offers customizable GPU server plans with DDoS protection and IPMI. Their pricing starts at £229 per month for the E5-2670V3 configuration with 2 x Xeon E5-2667 V3, 128 GB RAM, 2 x 960 GB SSD, and 2 x GPU Card. They also provide high-speed network with 1 Gbps connection and the latest NVIDIA, AMD, and Intel GPU technologies.

LeaderGPU

LeaderGPU offers high-performance GPU cards with flexible configurations. Their pricing starts at €2900.0 per month for the 6x3090, 6x24GB GPU, 2x6226R, 384GB RAM, NVLink configuration. They provide 24/7 customer service and certified data centers.

PQ Hosting

PQ Hosting offers stability under heavy loads and fast data processing. Their pricing starts at 4.77 € per month for the Aluminium-[NL] plan with basic protection against attacks and data loss, and NVMe & SSD drives. They also offer GPU technology for quick operations and customizable configurations.

CloudOYE

CloudOYE offers enhanced power and speed for computationally intensive activities. Their pricing starts at $600 per month for the Standard plan with 1 CPU, 1GB RAM, 30GB SSD Disk, and 1TB Data Transfer. They also provide multiple GPUs for complex computations and parallel processing.

Skystark

Skystark offers a range of cloud VPS with configurable cores, traffic, storage, and RAM. Their pricing starts at 2.99€ per month for the Cloud VPS XS plan with 1 core, 30 TB traffic, 15 GB NVMe SSD storage, and 1 GB RAM. They also offer unlimited traffic and competitive pricing for various server sizes.

Conclusion

In conclusion, cloud GPU providers offer a wide range of features and pricing options to cater to different use cases and budgets. Users should carefully consider their specific requirements, such as GPU models, memory, storage, and network capabilities, when selecting a cloud GPU service. It is important to evaluate the performance, scalability, security, and support offered by each provider to ensure the best fit for the intended application.
